Option Contracts

An option contract is defined as a derivative that provides the owner right, but not obligation, of selling or buying an underlying asset (like stock), at a pre-determined price (strike price) and on a specific date (expiration day).

There are two kind of option contracts in terms of the rights one trader might achive when she takes position in the market. Call and Pull options. A call option gives the holder right to buy underlying asset by a certain date for a certain price. A put option, on the other hand, gives the holder right of selling underlying asset by a certain date for a certain price. The price in the contract is known as the exercise price or strike price (K) and the date in the contract is known as the expiration date or maturity (T).

Each trader, based on the strategies she is choosing can take short or long position. As there is no free lunch on the market, higher risk means higher profit (or loos).
